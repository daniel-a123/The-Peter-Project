Orchestral Strings

The orchestral strings consist of 4 different types of instruments, the violin, the viola, the cello, and the bass. The bass is played standing up or sitting down and the instrument stands vertically on the ground. The bass plays the low parts and makes the chords and supports the orchestra. The cello, like the french horn, can play super low and super high. It plays the cool fun parts like the intro of Game of Thrones, or it can play chords like the bass. The viola is typically similar to the violin but plays the counter mellody. It plays the cool parts that are not the main song. And finally, the violin. It plays the main parts of songs and can play super high. This instrument usually has the largest section. All 4 instruments work together to create beautiful music and one instrument is never more important than the other. All 4 must work together as a team to make music.
